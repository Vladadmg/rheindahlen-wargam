After Action Report from your frontline correspondent


What can one say about this event? What superlatives can we employ?
The event seems to attract more and more new talent as well as acting as a spur to those who regularly attend. In Europe we all have to travel long distance but mention should be made of Arrigo who flew in from Bologna and Ki and his friend from Switzerland.
The centre piece was a massive 5 by 3 metre recreation of the Battle of the Little Bighorn for a 15mm scale battle. The cavalry battalions of Reno and Custer were at a one for one scale. However this mattered not as they still did not win! Not once! Well done to the Rheindahlen club for all their efforts and to Huw for painting god knows how many hundreds of figures for the battle.
Only a little smaller in size was a WWII recreation by Charles and Dougie in 1/48th scale. One of the new gaming areas to Action was Games Workshop with their Pirates which kept the boy population of Rheindahlen amused each day. Their (GW) painting techniques were also studied very attentively by most of the adults.
One of the more unusual games was a Vietnam recreation by Pete Garnham from the Apocalypse Now film. It was based on the surfing scene and was entitled Charlie Don’t Surf. It was accurately portrayed as Pete must have spent hours looking at the film frame by frame. It also used up most of the plastic flower arrangements found in Xenos. A good game and visually very attractive.
Our traders were most supportive as they have been in recent years. One of the newer traders was Richard and Zoë from Magister Militum and they seemed to have enjoyed themselves. SHQ came over from the UK and with Herr Hoffman and Mark Brown formed a massive mini mall at one end of the hall. We finally managed to increase our Mayhem range of ruined WWII buildings! Hopefully next year Herr Hoffman will bring along some of the Waterloo diorama he has put together. I have seen the photos and they look impressive. Or maybe we will go for the Gettysburg Diorama, another impressive piece of work which many people have contributed to.
We also had a first in that Card games were in evidence from Arne and his Warlord Saga. Will this be even bigger next year?
I have only mentioned a few names and a few games but this event would not be anything without the support and goodwill from all the people who do attend and in their own small way contribute to the success and continuation of Action. Next year is my last one as an organiser after that I shall have retired to the hills of Normandy to start my own Wargames club. I obviously want next year to be the biggest and the best we can present. I want lots of different games and themes, lots of people enjoying themselves and some really nice, mouth wateringly scenic presentations. Which brings me to mention one of our local traders, Didier, did you see his Lord of the Rings models? What quality!
Yes the standards are rising and in so many pleasant ways. Keep up the good work friends and let us see you all again next year.

Regards to all
Vic
